Hello! My name is Selena Kang.

Ever since downloading my first music video "LA chA TA" by f(x) onto my mp3 player in 2009 as an 8 year old child, I've always been attracted and impressed by the ever-changing tech world.

As a high school student, my favoite subject was math. Whatever the hardest course the school provided for math, I would take it. At the end of my senior year, I was the last girl remaining in the IB Mathematics HL class in addition to earning the Academic Achievement honor.

Coming into Cornell University, I applied to be an Information Science student, because I was interested in continuing the STEM field as well as exploring my interest in tech and society. I was certain I would follow a path where I could incroporate my STEM background into helping people in any way. From my freshman year, I took my first CS course in python, explored software engineering, web development, product design, UX research, and any career paths that I found that connected my interest in tech to people.

While speaking with professors, other students, and reflecting on myself, my interest in data and, more specifically, how data drives users rapidly grew. I am now an Operations Research and Engineering student pursuing a minor in Business, to learn more about people, and a minor in Information Science (concentration in Data Science), to gain interdisciplinary skills regarding data.

I am reflective, sociable, inquisitive, and curious to learn all I can including from the mistakes I make. I love working with people and have been part of various teams, including sports and academic teams.

I am currently looking for a position where I can work with data, interpreting and analyzing quantitative metrics to improve user experience.
